Must-Know Finance Terms

Here are the most important concepts explained simply:

  1. Compound Interest – "Interest on interest." The #1 reason money grows exponentially over time.
    • Rule of 72: Divide 72 by your interest rate to see how many years it takes to double your money
    • Example: $10,000 at 7% → ~$76,000 in 30 years
  2. Asset Allocation – How you divide your investments between stocks, bonds, cash, etc.
  3. Diversification – Don’t put all your eggs in one basket.
    • Stocks → Growth
    • Bonds → Stability
    • Real Estate → Income + appreciation
    • Cash → Emergency fund
  4. Net Worth = Assets – Liabilities
  5. Emergency Fund – 3–6 months of living expenses in cash or cash-equivalent
  6. 401(k) / IRA – Tax-advantaged retirement accounts (use them!)
  7. Index Fund – A low-cost way to own “the whole market” (recommended by Warren Buffett)
    1. S&P 500 index funds historically return ~10% per year
    2. Fees under 0.05% vs 1–2% for actively managed funds
